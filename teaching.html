Engineering Program Learning Objectives

EME 303 - Fluid Mechanics in Energy and Mineral Engineering

  1. Calculate pressure forces within hydrostatic fluid systems and extend these to the reaction forces and moments required to contain the fluid.
  2. Apply hydrostatic principles to convert manometer readings into measurements of fluid pressure.
  3. State the circumstances in which the Bernoulli equation is appropriate.
  4. Perform analysis of fluid problems using an integral control volume approach (Reynolds Transport Theorem), and perform momentum and energy balances on a fluid system.
  5. Conduct dimensional analysis to correctly scale experimental data to represent fluid phenomena in a "real" design.
  6. Identify important nondimensional parameters governing different types of fluid flow situations.
  7. Apply differential analysis to develop the governing equations for fluid flows.
  8. Perform calculations of viscous losses in pipe flows.
  9. Calculate lift and drag for airfoil and bluff bodies under external flow configurations.
  10. Solve open channel flow problems.

ME 300 - Engineering Thermodynamics I

  1. Apply the state principle to fix a thermodynamic state for ideal gases and steam.
  2. Obtain property data from steam tables, or from the NIST thermodynamic database.
  3. Apply theoretical state relations to calculate properties of ideal gases.
  4. Plot processes of ideal gases and multi-phase mixtures on common thermodynamic diagrams including P-v, T-v and T-s diagrams.
  5. Apply conservation principles, including mass and energy, to thermodynamic systems and correctly identify energy interactions across the boundaries.
  6. Apply conservation principles, including mass and energy, to thermodynamic control volumes and identify energy and mass interactions across the boundaries for common steady flow devices.
  7. Apply second law based analysis to calculate theoretical limits on thermal efficiency and coefficient of performance.
  8. Perform steady state analysis on systems operating on Rankine and refrigeration cycles, to compute work output and fix states around the cycle.
  9. Perform simple analysis of Brayton and Otto air standard cycles.

EGEE 437 - Design of Solar Energy Conversion Systems

  1. Mathematically describe basic radiative heat transfer phenomena and describe the process of energy exchange between the sun and the earth within this context.
  2. Predict the position of the sun in the sky throughout the astronomical year, explain the impact that this has on the solar irradiance on a fixed collector on the earth's surface, and conduct a solar site survey.
  3. Synthesize publicly available solar resource data to predict the energy production potential of solar energy collection systems in a given location.
  4. Perform simple economic analysis of solar energy collection systems, making business/financial calculations such as life cycle savings and return on investment.
  5. Describe the analytical approach for photovoltaic and optocaloric (solar thermal) solar energy systems and perform simple calculations.
